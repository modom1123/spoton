Why SpotOn. Why Dashboard. Why Me.

The Dashboard is where operators live.

Every SpotOn tool — Profit Assist, Teamwork, Reserve, Order — converges in the Dashboard. It's the back office that operators open first thing in the morning and last thing before close. I want to make it the reason they stay.

I've been the user.

I don't need personas to understand what an operator needs from a dashboard at 6 AM before open or midnight after close. I've pulled those reports. I've dug through sales mix data to figure out what went wrong on a bad shift. That intuition can't be replicated from user interviews alone.

I build for adoption, not features.

At IMPLAN, I led the shift from Sales-Led to Product-Led Growth — aligning product, pricing, and go-to-market around self-serve adoption. The Dashboard PM role is the same challenge: make powerful tools accessible enough that operators actually use them.

I find the money.

I've audited P&Ls, uncovered $200K in overages, and built reporting that surfaces what matters. SpotOn's own research says 84% of operators need better financial systems and 73% don't trust their pricing strategy. The Dashboard is where that trust gets built — or broken.

I care about the craft.

SpotOn's culture says "we treat you the way you treat your customers." I've spent my career at the intersection of operations and product because I believe the best tools are built by people who've felt the pain firsthand. Hospitality isn't a checkbox on my resume — it's the lens through which I do all my work.

How I build roadmaps

Votes are a signal. Weighted scoring is the decision.

I know SpotOn gives stakeholders the ability to vote on features — that's great because it builds transparency and buy-in. But voting alone can become a popularity contest. I layer data-driven prioritization methodologies on top to weigh each ask by business value, time criticality, and risk — so the roadmap reflects impact, not volume.
